# Pattern: Growth Bottleneck Map

## Applicable Situation

Use when growth is underperforming and the team needs to decide where to intervene first.

## Inputs

- Funnel or journey metrics
- Channel mix
- User or account quality data
- Conversion, activation, retention, expansion, or sales data
- Qualitative evidence from calls, support, reviews, or surveys

## Default Workflow

1. Map the journey: acquisition, qualification, activation, conversion, retention, expansion, advocacy.
2. Identify where volume, quality, or conversion breaks.
3. Separate product-value problems from traffic-quality and motion-design problems.
4. Compare the likely leverage of fixing each bottleneck.
5. Choose one bottleneck and propose experiments with clear success metrics.

## Branch Rules

- If free-to-paid conversion is low, inspect acquisition quality before assuming product onboarding is broken.
- If channels are noisy or collapsing, look for defensible growth loops and trust-based signals.
- If sales conversion is low, inspect ICP, champion, status quo, and sales narrative.
- If activation is low for good-fit users, defer to Product for product experience changes.

## Anti-Patterns

- Treating conversion rate as one isolated number.
- Improving product before checking whether the right users are arriving.
- Running channel experiments without ICP or success criteria.

## Acceptance Criteria

- The diagnosis names one primary bottleneck and supporting evidence.
- The recommendation distinguishes GTM fixes from Product fixes.
- The next experiments can be measured.

## Conflict Notes

Some sources frame distribution as the new bottleneck; others emphasize product retention. Diagnose from evidence and avoid assuming either side.
